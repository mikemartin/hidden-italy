short_bio: 'I first went to Italy as a backpacker in 1980 and fell in love with the country. I returned in 1983, living and working in Milan until 1988. Afterwards I worked at the Institute of Languages, University of New South Wales (Sydney) taking guided tours to Italy annually. I spent two years with my family in Prato (Florence) in 2003 and 2004, working at the Monash University (Melbourne) Centre. Since then I have dedicated myself full-time to Hidden Italy. I love photography and writing and have had articles and photographs on Italy published in most major Australian newspapers and travel magazines.   In 2025 I had a book published called the Trails to Freedom, based on the first time I did this solo Alpine hike (which is now the basis for one of our most popular tours).  I speak fluent Italian, alas with a pronounced Australian accent.'
