After finishing school, she studied natural sciences at the University of Perugia and graduated with a PhD in biology. In 2018, she became an environmental hiking guide and has combined this with her research and teaching responsibilities.         text: "It was over two hundred years ago that Napoleon disembarked at Portoferraio to start his exile on Elba, a small island off the Tuscan coast. They were different times, and his English captors were remarkably deferential, installing Napoleon (with his entourage) effectively as the lord of the island. He only stayed for 10 months before escaping in a small boat (despite the close surveillance by Sir Neil Campbell and his troops) and reviving his imperial ambitions. His presence, however, left a strong memory on this pretty island and there are many traces of his time here, but a visit to Elba, well-merited even without the Great Man.\_"

        text: "Elba is Italy’s third largest island (29km long and 19km wide). It has exceptionally clear water, fine, white-sanded beaches and a lush wooded interior superb for walking and even in the height of summer, inland villages remain mostly quiet. Portoferraio is very much the capital, a colourful port overlooked by a warren of old alleys. Elsewhere the most attractive towns are Capolieri and Porto Azzurro in the southeast and Marciana in the west, the last of these offering access to woodland hikes and an impressive chairlift up to Mt Capanne (100m). Biodola occupies an idyllic sweeping bay near Portoferraio. You can also take ferries to some of the other islands of the little archipelago.\_"

Driving towards the coast from here you come to the hamlet of Cinque Cannelle, where until 30 years ago, local women came to wash the clothes. There is an excellent and very relaxed ristorante here for lunch, the Bar Ristorante da Cipolla. After lunch you can carry on to Rio Marina on the coast, which was where the minerals were loaded onto ships.'
