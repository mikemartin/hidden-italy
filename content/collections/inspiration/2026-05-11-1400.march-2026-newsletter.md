        type: text
        text: "Campania is a mountainous promontory surrounded by miles of coastline, sixty kilometres south of Salerno.\_ Little known to foreign tourists, the Cilento is much loved by Italians, particularly in summer when its picturesque towns, sandy beaches and hiking trails draw people from all over the peninsula.\_"
  -
    type: paragraph
    content:
      -
        type: text
        text: "The Cilento is a magical place of crystalline sea, imposing mountains and wild forests; it has several important archaeological sites; and, last but not least, the Cilento is renown for the quality of its agricultural products.\_ It is also well-known for its wild herbs that, according to many people, hold the secret to longevity.\_"
  -
    type: paragraph
    content:
      -
        type: text
        text: "Be sceptical but and it was no coincidence that the concept of the ‘Mediterranean Diet’ was actually born here in the Cilento.\_ The American nutritionist and physiologist Ancel Keys lived and worked in the small coastal Pioppi for over forty years and used the results of his studies to develop his dietary concepts.\_ Over time, Pioppi became known as the birthplace of Mediterranean food culture and is now home to the Museum of the Mediterranean Diet."
  -
    type: paragraph
    content:
      -
        type: text
        text: "Most of the Cilento Peninsula is under the tutelage of the National Park of Cilento, Vallo di Diano and Alburni.\_ The park covers most of the hinterland, which includes several mountains over two thousand metres high as well as the coastline and a large tract of the seafloor, protecting marine areas: the Casota del Infreschi in the south and the Costa di Castellabate in the north."

        text: "Castellabate is a good place to start your exploration of the Cilento.\_ It has a spectacular location overlooking the coast and it is easy to lose yourself poking around the paved lanes and squares of the town.\_ A long flight of stairs lead up to the town’s castle, which dates from 1123 and then onto the basilica of Santa Maria de Giulia, which also dates from the 12th century.\_ Beyond this is the ‘belvedere’ of San Costabile, which has a magnificent terrace with views out across the Tyrrhenian Sea, with the Sorrentine Peninsula and the islands of Capri in the distance."

    type: paragraph

        type: text
        text: "From Olgiastro Marina, the road continues south past more beaches to the pretty fishing village of Acciaroli, where Ernest Hemingway stayed for several weeks during his Italian sojourn between 1950 and 1951.\_ Here the great man met a local fisherman, Antonio Masarone, who was known as ‘u viecchiu’, or the Old Man in the dialect.\_ Many locals believe that Antonio was the inspiration for Hemingway’s Nobel Prize winning novel, The Old Man and The Sea.\_ But Acciaroli greatest claim to fame is the Spiaggia Grande, the most popular beach on the whole coast in summer but virtually deserted in winter. \_"
  -
    type: paragraph
    content:

        type: text
        text: "A short drive on is the town of Pioppi, which also has a number of nice beaches nearby, but is best known for its Mediterranean Diet Study Centre, which is located in the 16th century Palazzo Principi in the centre of the town.\_ The centre hosts a research facility and holds regular conferences and cooking course which propagate the benefits the Mediterranean diet.\_ There is also a museum dedicated to the Diet and another in the palazzo called the Living Museum of the Sea, both worth a visit."
  -
    type: paragraph
    content:
      -
        type: text
        text: "From Pioppi you can continue further south along the SS 167 to Marina di Ascae, which has Velia, a splendid Greco-Roman archaeological site overlooking the sea. \_Founded by Greek colonists in the 6th century BC, Velia was a prosperous part of Magna Graecia. It gained importance as a commercial centre and gave birth to two important philosophers Parmenides and Zeno. Velia was home to a prestigious medical school. In Roman times, illustrious figures such as Cicero and Horace found Velia an ideal destination for their vacations. Velia declined with the gradual silting up of the ports, which meant the end of commercial wealth.\_ Today, Velia is a popular tourist destination, and visitors can explore the ruins of the city's temples, baths, agora, and other structures. \_The acropolis is the highest point of Velia and was home to the city's most important temples and religious structures.\_ It is also a fabulous place to finish your day, watching the sunset over the gulf."

        text: 'Sunday, option 1: Go for a hike'
  -
    type: paragraph
    content:
      -
        type: text
        text: "Monte Gelbison (1705 mts asl) is in the centre of the National Park.\_ There are several routes to the top of the mountain but the best known is the Sentiero del Pellegrino (the Pilgrims' Path).\_ The trail is of considerable historical and religious importance, featuring a well-maintained path that alternates paved and dirt paths.\_ It starts in Novi Velia and leads to the evocative sanctuary of the Madonna del Monte Sacro at the summit of mountain.\_ It is a ten to twelve kilometres round-trip with a significant elevation gain.\_ It passes through beech forests, offering panoramic views of the Cilento, the sea, and nearby Mount Cervati."
  -
    type: paragraph
    content:
      -
        type: text
        text: "Monte Bulgheria (1225 mts asl) is at the southern end of the park, above the town of Scario.\_ The Sentiero degli Eremiti (The Hermits' Path) is a fascinating hiking trail that starts from the village San Giovanni. It is characterised by lush vegetation and its historical ties to the ascetic communities that settled on the mountain in the Middle Ages.\_ The hike takes from 4 to 5 hours, out and back, with an elevation gain of 700 meters.\_ It passes the remains of several ancient hermitages along the way.\_ The summit offers spectacular panoramic views over the Gulf of Policastro."

        text: "Paestum is twenty kilometres north of Castellabate.\_ It is one of the most important and evocative archaeological sites in southern Italy.\_ The ruins of three magnificent gold-stone Doric temples that stand on a large bare plain, the Pian del Sele, are amongst the best preserved in Europe, the remains of a Greek city founded in the 6th century.\_ Later abandoned because of Saracen raids and malaria the site was only rediscovered in the 18th century (Shelley called it ‘impressively grand’). \_It is a spectacular place, enhanced with an excellent museum, which has displays of Greek and Roman finds from the site and around.\_"
