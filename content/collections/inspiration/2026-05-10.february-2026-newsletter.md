        text: "Few people know that in the middle of the Mediterranean, in the heart of Calabria, a region we associate with sun, sea and kilometres of sandy beaches, there is a hidden place that could have come straight out of The Call of the Wild, area full of wolves; high plains; extensive forests of pine, larch, maples and beech trees; and vast expanses of fresh snow.\_ Less than an hour’s drive from some of the most beautiful coastline in Italy, National Park of the Sila covers 150,000 hectares of protected mountain landscapes and is divided between three provinces, Cosenza, Crotone and Catanzaro.\_ More Scandi than southern Italy.\_"

        text: "The oldest traces of human presence here date back to at least 3,500 BC, to a Neolithic village whose archaeological remains were discovered a few years ago on the southern shore of Lake Cecita.\_ In Greek and Roman times, the Sila later became the mythical home of Silvanus or Silenus, son of the Crati River and God of the forest, which both Virgil and Plinio wrote about.\_ Under the Byzantines, the Sila became a refuge for throngs of Basilian monks from the Balkans and Greece.\_\_"
  -
    type: paragraph
    content:
      -
        type: text
        text: "The Byzantines were followed by the Normans, and then by the chaos of Swabian, Angevin, Aragonese, and Bourbon rule.\_ Centuries of war, famine, earthquakes, and banditry, however, failed to tarnish the beauty of these places and fortunately this extraordinary territory and unique mountain massif rising in the heart of the Mediterranean, with its forests, valleys, rivers, waterfalls, giant trees, cliffs, canyons, villages, and testimonies of history, art, and traditions, is once again regaining the fame that classical authors rightly assigned to it.\_ It is a perfect place for a winter escape!\_"
