        text: "Italian drivers have an unfair reputation as bad drivers.\_ On the contrary, I think they are very skillful and attentive - the difficulty is that they operate from a different paradigm.\_ Like other aspects of Italian life, driving rules tend to be treated with a certain flexibility, eg respecting give way signs, lane markers, pedestrian crossings (never assume that they will stop for you), parking signs etc.\_ As a visitor, however, the onus is on us to adapt to their style rather than expecting to conform to ours."

        text: "Because of the narrow, crowded streets, complicated one-way streets and limited parking, I feel it is best to avoid driving in the cities.\_ My tip is, where possible, to pick up and drop off your hire car at the airport and use the shuttle service to get in and out of town."

        text: "Italy has an extensive freeway system, the autostrade (green signs) which is an excellent way to get from A to B, although it can be a little boring.\_ The 120 km per hour speed limit is often exceeded (and can seem more like a minimum than a maximum speed).\_ I'd suggest only using the outer fast lane only to overtake.\_ On a three lane road, I'd suggest sticking in the middle lane!"
  -
    type: paragraph
    content:
      -
        type: text
        text: "Be prepared for congestion on the autostradas that circle the big cities during morning and evening peak hours, as they are often used by commuters.\_ If possible, plan longer trips for Sundays and public holidays, as semi-trailers and heavy transports aren't allowed on the autostradas on these days."
